Joan Ganz Cooney

Early Life

Joan Ganz Cooney, born on November 30, 1929, is the cofounder of the hit children series, Sesame Street. She grew up in an upper middle class family, often participating in school plays in high school. She pursued acting, but later in college she chose teaching because it was "acceptable" for a woman back then. After graduating college in 1951, she moved to Washington DC, where she worked a series of many jobs before she cofounded Sesame Street.

Sesame Street's Impact

Sesame Street was the first preschool show that intergrated both entertainment and education into a program, and its become the longest running children's show in U.S. history. Millions of children around the world have watched and loved this show, becoming a cultural icon in the eyes of many.
